Body Scan Meditation Technique:

  1. Begin by lying down or sitting in a comfortable chair. If lying down, let your arms and legs relax and fall to the sides; if seated, find a balanced and stable position.
  2. Take a few moments to notice sensations of breathing.
  3. Draw your attention to your feet. Notice the pressure of your feet against the floor or bed, the temperature, comfort or discomfort, itches, or anything else. Expect your mind to wander, and when it does, return your attention to your feet without judging yourself or giving yourself a hard time. Let your attention rest with your feet in this way for a few minutes.
  4. Move attention to your lower legs. You might feel the touch of clothing or a blanket, and you might feel nothing at all. Sustain your attention without rigidly exhausting yourself. Whatever you experience, that's what you're supposed to feel right now.
  5. After a few minutes, shift attention to your upper legs, observing them in the same way.
  6. Pacing yourself turn this same kind of attention to your abdomen and then to your chest. Notice physical sensations, such as breathing, internal feelings like hunger or fullness, and the resonance of any emotionsâ€”physical manifestations of happiness, sadness, tension, anger, feeling open or closed, and so on.
  7. Continue turning attention to the rest of your body in the same way, spending several minutes each on your back, then your hands, then your arms. Then bring attention to your neck and shoulders, releasing tension when you're able without fighting what remains.
  8. Finally, bring attention to your face and head, noticing expressions and reflections of emotions that occur around your mouth and eyes in particular.
  9. Whether you feel relaxed or tense, restless or invigorated, pause before concluding. Take a moment of stillness, and then, with intention, choose when to move on with your day.

Awareness of the Breath Technique:

  1. Find a comfortable seated position. You can sit on a chair with your feet flat on the ground, or on a cushion on the floor with your legs crossed.
  2. Sit up straight, but not rigid. Allow your spine to be long and your shoulders to relax.
  3. Close your eyes or lower your gaze to reduce visual distractions.
  4. Take a few deep breaths to help your body settle into the position.
  5. Now, allow your breath to return to its natural rhythm. Don't try to control it; simply observe it as it is.
  6. Focus your attention on the sensation of breathing. You might notice the air moving in and out of your nostrils, or the rise and fall of your chest or abdomen.
  7. When you notice your mind has wandered (and it will), gently bring your attention back to your breath. Do this without judgment or frustration.
  8. Continue this practice for your desired duration, whether it's 5 minutes or longer.
  9. When you're ready to end, slowly open your eyes and take a moment to notice how you feel before moving on with your day.

Loving Kindness Meditation Technique:

  1. Find a comfortable position and close your eyes.
  2. Take a few deep breaths to center yourself.
  3. Bring to mind someone you care about deeply.
  4. Silently repeat phrases of loving-kindness towards them:
    • "May you be happy"
    • "May you be healthy"
    • "May you be safe"
    • "May you live with ease"
  5. Next, direct these phrases towards yourself.
  6. Gradually extend these wishes to others: friends, neutral people, difficult people, and all beings.
  7. End by taking a few deep breaths and slowly opening your eyes.

Complete Meditation Instructions:

  1. Find a comfortable seated position.
  2. Close your eyes or maintain a soft gaze.
  3. Take a few deep breaths to settle in.
  4. Begin to notice your breath without changing it.
  5. Observe the sensations of breathing throughout your body.
  6. When your mind wanders, gently bring attention back to the breath.
  7. Expand awareness to include body sensations.
  8. Notice any thoughts or emotions that arise.
  9. Practice observing without judgment or attachment.
  10. Gradually expand awareness to sounds in the environment.
  11. Maintain an open, receptive state of mind.
  12. When ready, slowly open your eyes and return to the present moment.
